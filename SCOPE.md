# Scope and Boundaries

## In scope through Phase 14

- Industry-neutral canonical and technical/control-plane models
- Organization scoping, effective dates, and versioned metadata
- Strict configuration and API contracts, migrations, and seed metadata
- Deterministic synthetic multi-system fixtures and identity truth data
- Read-only CSV, XLSX, JSON, Parquet, REST, and PostgreSQL extraction
- Source connection tests and schema discovery
- Immutable raw snapshots, manifests, SHA-256 checksums, schema fingerprints, cursors, idempotency, bounded retry, resume, and safe failure metadata
- Masked schema/data profiling, persisted baselines, and configurable drift evidence
- Safe versioned mapping DSL execution, lookups, previews, errors, diffs, and plugins
- Multi-category validation, transparent quality dimensions, quarantine, resolution, and reprocessing history
- Deterministic organization-scoped identity linkage, conflict detection, reversible decisions, and synthetic truth evaluation
- Probabilistic blocking/scoring, explicit synthetic estimation, gray-zone review evidence, and model/run attribution
- Run/field lineage, OpenLineage-compatible events, governed marts, masked CSV/Parquet exports, checksums, data dictionaries, and retention
- Asset dependencies, schedules, partitions, retries, failure hooks, watermarks, change hashes, incremental skips, backfills, and telemetry
- Isolated demo identity, OIDC-ready claims conversion, organization/permission authorization, audit events, security headers, request/rate limits, and retention enforcement
- Strict documentation site, evaluator/developer paths, synthetic screenshots, and synthetic walkthrough video
- Versioned benchmark and budgets, SBOMs, dependency/secret scans, Python packages, release checksums, pinned CI, container scan/sign hooks, and release-review evidence
- Reproducible backend/frontend toolchains, CI, tests, and documentation

## Out of scope after blueprint completion

- Claiming a complete production deployment or partner-validated result
- Treating synthetic probabilistic thresholds as production-calibrated guarantees
- Automatic source-system writes
- Production identity provider integration
- Compliance certification
- Consequential decisions about employment, eligibility, admissions, discipline, disability, or services
- Any claim of company adoption or measured business improvement

## Public/private boundary

Public: source code, generic adapters, synthetic fixtures, schemas, tests, diagrams, documentation, and generic pilot materials.

Private: partner data, credentials, endpoints, mappings, business rules, screenshots, results, and proprietary adapters unless the owner explicitly authorizes release.
